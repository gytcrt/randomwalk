Like most startups, we approached a particular problem space with an initial hypothesis and began validating that hypothesis with potential buyers and users. In our case, that problem space is customer service. Though many people may assume it's an overcrowded market, we believe there are still unaddressed needs. 

My cofounder and I talked to more than fifty customer experience leaders and practitioners across industries. Here are nine takeaways from the conversations.

## The future of customer experience (CX) is hybrid

1. **Simple tasks will be automated by self-service systems or AI agents; human interaction and problem solving remain critical to many companies. Except for some use cases like restaurant reservation and ordering, we expect a hybrid model of AI agents and human agents in the coming years.**

	Chain restaurants and even independent restaurants have experienced the benefit of implementing AI agents into their reservation and ordering system. In the past, they were limited by the number of staff that take calls during peak hours; many calls were missed due to staff's capacity. However, since reservations and ordering can be generally standardized, AI agents can easily take them at scale. The consistency of service provided by AI agents also makes customers happier—they don't care who takes their call as long as their tables are reserved.  

	Nevertheless, most other use cases require more human interaction. In the adjacent industry of food delivery service, many anxious customers call in or start a chat to check the status of their order. AI agents can easily give a customer the latest status of their order, but it does not necessarily solve the emotional need of a hungry and angry customer. The person might churn out of frustration, which can be especially dire to the business if the customer has a high lifetime value(LTV). In those cases, businesses need to make wise decisions on what to automate and what to channel to more empathetic and creative human agents. 

2. **Many CX organizations will start operating with AI agents, in-house CX team, and BPO partners across multiple channels. The operational complexity will be more challenging than it used to be.**

	CX leaders need to decide and consistently evaluate:
	- What type of problems should be automated by AI without hurting customer experience?
	- How to predict the demand for BPO teams and manage performance across multiple BPO partners.
	- How to measure performance and ROI across different channels and providers.

	Classic metrics like AHT, CSAT or NPS do not answer those questions for CX leaders, since they reflect the results rather than the causes, nor do they provide a clear direction on the next steps. 

## CX is still incorrectly treated as a cost center

3. **The current focus of CX is on dealing with issues in the fastest and cheapest way, but it should be linked to product, sales and retention.**

	Product teams, customer care teams, and customer success teams would love to have bugs, churn signals, and customer pain quickly surfaced from customer interactions with the CX team. However, very few companies have enabled close collaboration between those teams and CX team due to overly emphasizing on speed and cost of CX operation. In addition, promptly and accurately extracting valuable signals from customer interaction at scale is not easy. 

## Lack of visibility and limitation on data

4. **Understanding of customer interaction data including chat, call, and email is limited since most organizations still rely on limited QA and survey for operational insights and customer feedback.**

	Currently, most companies QA less than 5% of their total CX interactions with customers. Many of them sample single digits number of interactions per agent per week for quality and training purposes. Even though almost all customer interactions data are captured by businesses, the majority of them are never analyzed, largely due to lack of resources or technology to understand them. 

	Surveys are another way to measure service quality and collect customer feedback. Many widely used metrics in CX like NPS and CSAT come from survey data. However, survey data suffer significantly on two aspects:

	* On average, 90% of customers don't answer CX related surveys; the ones who respond do not present the whole customer population. This makes analysis on NPS and CSAT highly skewed to the subset of customers who responded to the surveys.   
	* Getting feedback from surveys usually takes weeks to months, during which companies cannot act quickly on product faults or process oversights. We have heard over and over again from CX leaders that they want to know the problems faster. 

5. **Organizational data silo leads to further operational friction across teams.** 

	This challenge manifests in multiple ways:

	* Getting operational insights and customer feedback becomes even harder and slower.   
	* Before investing and moving towards a hybrid operation model, companies with severe data silo issues sometimes spend months to years to build a master data.  
	* The CX team takes the hit when issues originated from other teams and the CX team does not have the context.

## Tooling continues being a challenge 

6. **Human agents struggle with poor toolings—in many cases, they need to operate on ten different systems to help one customer. Ideally, their AHT will automatically go down and their work experience will improve if companies can fix the tooling issues. But companies that treat CX as a cost center do not want to invest.** 

7. **Outdated documentation and SOPs hurt both human agent performance and AI agent adoption.** 

	We have heard complaints from frontline staff about dated documentation and SOPs—they cannot cut their AHT down when they have to present unnecessary information to customers based on the SOP, nor can they effectively help a customer when the issue is new and not updated in their reference documentation.  
	  
	AI agent adoption will suffer from dated documentation and SOPs to an even larger degree. Humans operate with creativity and flexibility, but AI relies on data and rules you feed to it.   

## Frontline staff's struggles

8. **Frontline managers/team leaders are frequently underwater—they want to invest in coaching their team members and providing good experience, but given their workload and the amount of unstructured data, providing targeted and example based coaching to their team members is challenging.**   
     
9. **Dissatisfaction is a common experience and turnover rate is high among CX front line workers. Training someone to be an empathetic problem solver for your company requires investment. As CX operation moves to hybrid mode, human agents will deal with mostly complex issues, which requires a deep understanding of the business and interpersonal dynamics.**

## Conclusion

I really enjoyed talking to people from different industries and working on different types of CX problems—some of them manage a support team of over a thousand people for B2B businesses, and some lead a remote team of eight people. It's intellectually stimulating to collect feedback from them, and distill the information into themes and product features.

I will continue sharing more insights as we build our product. As always, feedback is welcomed.
